Model Evaluation Canvas

Help leaders evaluate vendor model claims and internal pilot results without writing any code. Use during vendor demos, pilot review meetings, and pre-deployment sign-offs. Pairs with the /eval-design and /review skills in this workspace.

1. Before the meeting — pre-fill the context

2. The 7 questions to ask in the meeting

Q1. What baseline did you compare to?

Why ask: If they can't tell you, the headline accuracy is meaningless.
Verdict pattern: If lift over baseline is < 5% on the metric that drives the decision, the engineering and maintenance cost rarely justifies shipping. No baseline given = stop the meeting.

Q2. What metric did you optimize, and why that one?

Why ask: Accuracy hides the failure modes that hurt your business. Wrong metric → great-looking model that destroys value.
Pattern: Fraud, cancer screening → recall matters. Spam, ad targeting → precision matters. "Accuracy" alone on imbalanced classes is a tell of a weak presentation.

Q3. Show me the confusion matrix

Why ask: A single accuracy number hides whether errors fall on the rare-but-expensive class.
Pattern: 99% accuracy on a 99/1 class = the trivial "always say no" model. Demand class-level recall numbers. No confusion matrix on imbalanced data = the model is hiding something.

Q4. How did you split train, validation, and test?

Why ask: Train/test leakage is the #1 cause of "model great in dev, garbage in production."
Pattern: Time-series problem with random split = optimistic accuracy. Customer-level prediction with same customer in train + test = leakage. "Random split" on time-series data is a red flag.

Q5. Where does the training data come from, and is it representative?

Why ask: Training distribution ≠ production distribution = silent failure on day 1.
Pattern: Model trained on Tier-1 customers tested on Tier-3 = wrong segments = will not generalize. Public-benchmark-only models almost always under-perform on enterprise data.

Q6. What does the failure mode look like?

Why ask: "Where it fails" tells you more than "where it succeeds." Look for: bias on protected groups, drift on rare cases, edge-case behavior.
Pattern: A 92% overall accuracy with 75% accuracy on women / minorities / SMB segment is unshippable in regulated domains and dangerous in any domain. No error analysis = vendor doesn't know where it breaks.

Q7. How will the model degrade, and what's the kill switch?

Why ask: Models drift silently. Without monitoring + a kill switch, you only learn it broke when customers complain.
Pattern: No drift monitoring + no kill switch = you bought a black box you can't safely operate. Both must exist before production.

3. The 12 red flags checklist

Tick what you observed. 0–2 = green; 3–5 = yellow; 6+ = stop.

No baseline shown
Accuracy is the only metric reported
No confusion matrix despite imbalanced classes
Random split on time-dependent data
Same entities in train and test
Training data is public benchmark only
No subgroup / fairness analysis on regulated decision
No error analysis or example failures shown
Performance reported in % only — no $ or hours-saved
"We can't share the test set / methodology"
No drift monitoring plan
No kill switch / fallback path

4. The 5 sign-off requirements

All must be ✅ before approving production deployment.

RequirementWhat "ready" looks like
1. Defined decisionSpecific action triggered by prediction; threshold set; not "informational only"
2. Baseline beatenDocumented baseline; lift > 5% on the metric that matters
3. Right metricPrecision/recall/F1 chosen with explicit business reasoning, not "accuracy because it's familiar"
4. Held-out test untouchedTest set was scored ONCE at the end; not used to tune anything
5. Drift monitor + kill switchBoth exist; on-call owner named; weekly eval set in place

5. Sample questions vendors love and how to redirect

Vendor leans on…Better question
"State-of-the-art benchmarks""What did you score on a 100-example sample of *our* data?"
"Trained on billions of tokens""What's the recency, source, and bias profile of the training data?"
"Same architecture as a frontier model""What's the lift over a logistic regression baseline on our task?"
"Customers love it" / case studies"Show me the confusion matrix from one of those customers"
"It learns from your data""What's the retention policy and can we opt out of training?"
