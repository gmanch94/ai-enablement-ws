Fairness Metric Selection

▶

Select the primary fairness criterion for this system. Only one can be optimized at a time. Document the choice and rationale.

Demographic Parity (Statistical Parity)
Model selects equal proportions across demographic groups regardless of base rates. Best for: equalizing access, top-of-funnel screening.
Equalized Odds
Equal true positive AND false positive rates across groups. Best for: when ground-truth outcomes are reliable and errors matter symmetrically.
Equal Opportunity
Equal true positive rates across groups (same recall). Best for: ensuring qualified candidates from all groups have equal chance of being selected.
Individual Fairness
Similar individuals receive similar scores. Best for: when group-level statistics are less meaningful than case-by-case consistency.
Calibration (Predictive Parity)
Model scores mean the same probability across groups. Best for: risk scoring tools where score interpretation must be consistent.
⚠️ Fairness Incompatibility (Northpoint / ProPublica, 2016)

Demographic parity, equalized odds, and calibration cannot all be satisfied simultaneously when base rates differ between groups. This is a mathematical constraint, not a modeling failure. Acknowledge this tradeoff explicitly. Reference: Chouldechova (2017), Kleinberg et al. (2017).
