AI Bias Audit Template

General-purpose bias audit · Pre-deployment template for ML models affecting people

Complete before deploying any ML model that affects people. Aligned with NIST AI RMF (MAP/MEASURE/MANAGE), EU AI Act high-risk system requirements, and EEOC 4/5ths rule. For employment-specific use cases, prefer bias-audit-hr.html.

1
Deployment Context
Identify the model, its use case, and who is affected.
2
Training Data Assessment
Data Risks (NIST AI RMF MEASURE) — pre-existing bias in historical data is the most common source of algorithmic bias.
Pre-existing bias: Training data that reflects past human decisions (hiring, lending, sentencing) encodes those decisions' biases. The model learns the pattern — including the discriminatory one. Example: Amazon's hiring model trained on 10 years of resumes penalized women because historical hiring was male-dominated.
Does the training data represent all affected demographic groups proportionally?
Does the training data reflect historically discriminatory decisions (hiring, lending, criminal justice, etc.)?
Were any external datasets or data marketplace purchases used in training?
3
Feature & Proxy Variable Analysis
Excluding protected attributes is not enough — proxy variables can encode discrimination indirectly.
Proxy variables: Features that correlate with protected attributes reproduce bias even when the protected attribute is excluded. Examples: zip code → race (historical redlining), job title gaps → gender, "years since graduation" → age.
Were any features removed because they are protected attributes or direct proxies?
Disparate impact screen: does any remaining feature produce materially different outcomes across demographic groups?
4
Fairness Metric Selection
Different fairness definitions are mathematically incompatible — you must choose one and document the trade-off.
The incompatibility theorem: Individual fairness (similar people get similar outcomes) and group fairness (equal outcome rates across groups) cannot both be satisfied simultaneously except in degenerate cases. Choosing a metric is a value judgment, not a technical one.
Acknowledgment: individual and group fairness cannot both be fully satisfied; the above represents a deliberate choice with documented trade-offs.
5
Bias Testing Results
Testing & Trust — performance must be measured across demographic groups, not just in aggregate.
Group Accuracy % False Positive Rate % False Negative Rate % Disparate Impact Ratio
4/5ths rule (EEOC Uniform Guidelines): A disparate impact ratio below 0.80 for any protected group may constitute unlawful discrimination in US employment and credit contexts. EU AI Act high-risk systems require documented bias testing.
6
Mitigation Actions
Document what was done to address identified bias at each stage of the ML pipeline.
7
Human Oversight & Transparency
Controls and transparency requirements — calibrated to risk level per governance framework.
Human review checkpoints defined before consequential decisions are executed?
Individuals informed that AI was used in a decision affecting them?
Individuals can request an explanation of the decision on request?
Meaningful appeal or reconsideration mechanism exists?

Compliance / Regulatory — document applicable law and obtain required approvals.
