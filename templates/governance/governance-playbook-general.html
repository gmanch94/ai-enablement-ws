Step 1 — Classify Model Risk Level

Answer three questions. The appropriate checklist will be automatically selected.
Does this model make or significantly influence decisions affecting people's employment, finances, health, housing, or legal status?
Examples: hiring/promotion/termination, loan approval, medical diagnosis, bail/sentencing, insurance underwriting
Does this model use sensitive personal data or proxy variables that may encode protected characteristics?
Proxy examples: zip code → race/income, name patterns → ethnicity/gender, browsing history → health status
Will decisions be largely automated with limited human review, or deployed at high volume (>10,000 decisions/month)?
High volume + low oversight = higher risk of systematic harm before detection

Model Card (Google Standard — required for high risk)

For each high-risk deployed model, maintain a Model Card documenting the following. Model cards enable auditability and accountability across the full deployment lifecycle.

Architecture, training approach, version
Intended use and explicit non-uses
Relevant demographic and environmental factors
Evaluation metrics and thresholds
Evaluation data: collection method, diversity
Training data: what was used and why
Disaggregated results by demographic subgroup
Ethical considerations and sensitive use cases
Caveats, limitations, and monitoring requirements
