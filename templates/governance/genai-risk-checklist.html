GenAI Pre-Deployment Risk Checklist

3-question risk classifier auto-selects applicable tier. Tick what's done. Don't ship until your critical items are green.

Risk Tier Classifier

Tier: MEDIUM

1. Hallucination Controls

Users are warned that outputs may be wrong
Set expectations. Always required.
RAG or grounding is implemented for factual queries
Reduces hallucination by tying answers to retrieved sources.
Outputs cite sources where possible
Lets users verify claims.
Human review required before any external-facing output is published
Last line of defense for high-stakes contexts.
Confidence scores or "I don't know" responses are surfaced to users
Prevents over-trust on uncertain answers.

2. Bias & Fairness

Team has reviewed known bias risks of the chosen foundation model
Vendors publish model cards / evaluation reports.
Outputs tested across protected demographic groups
Look for disparate quality, refusals, or tone.
Redteaming run with adversarial / edge-case prompts
Surface failure modes before users do.
Formal bias audit completed by independent reviewer
Required for regulated decisions (ECOA, hiring law, etc.).
Adverse-action / explainability mechanism in place
Required by law in regulated decisions.

3. Privacy & Data Protection

Vendor's data retention + training-on-input policy reviewed
Default: opt out of training-on-input for any business use.
PII scrubbing/masking on prompts before vendor API call
Don't ship raw PII to third-party APIs.
Logged prompts + outputs stored with encryption + access controls
Same standard as any sensitive data.
Data residency requirements satisfied (EU, regulated regions)
May force EU-hosted endpoints or self-hosted models.
DPIA / privacy impact assessment completed
Required under GDPR for high-risk processing.

4. IP & Plagiarism

Vendor indemnifies for IP claims on generated output
All major providers offer this — verify it covers your use case.
Output plagiarism / similarity check process defined
Especially for content you publish or sell.
Attribution policy for AI-assisted content published externally
Increasingly required by law and platforms.
Code outputs scanned for license-incompatible snippets
Avoid GPL contamination in proprietary codebases.

5. Operational Reliability

Cost per call + per month tracked
LLM costs runaway easily.
Latency monitored; user-facing timeout defined
LLM responses can stall — degrade gracefully.
Fallback model or graceful failure path
Vendor outages happen. Have a Plan B.
Quality monitoring + drift alerts in production
Models change behavior across versions.
Multi-vendor abstraction (LangChain / LiteLLM / custom)
Avoid vendor lock-in for critical paths.
Incident response plan for AI-specific failures
Hallucinations + bias incidents need documented playbooks.

6. Governance & Sign-off

Use case logged in AI inventory
You can't govern what you don't track.
Legal review completed
Vendor terms, liability, jurisdiction.
Security review completed (data flow, access controls)
Same bar as any third-party integration.
Executive sponsor signed off on residual risk
Document that leadership accepts what remains.
Compliance / regulatory review (per applicable framework: EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF, sector-specific)
Regulator-facing audit trail.
