LLM Vendor Comparison Framework

Procurement support for enterprise leaders. Compare frontier closed-source (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google) vs. open-weight (Meta Llama, Mistral, DeepSeek) on the dimensions that matter for enterprise buying. Vendor capability data sourced from public vendor documentation; verify against current product pages before contract.

1. The 4 buying questions

QuestionWhy it mattersWhere to look
Will this vendor exist in 3 years?Switching cost is real. Lock-in via fine-tunes, prompts, and tools.Funding, revenue, enterprise customer count
Can I get my data back out?Fine-tunes and embeddings can be vendor-trapped.Export terms, model weight portability, BYOK
What happens to my prompts?Default-on training is the silent risk.Data retention, training opt-out, zero-retention SKU
Can I host it myself if I have to?Some industries (banking, defense, healthcare) require it.Open weights, VPC deployment, on-prem option

6. Anti-patterns

PatternWhy it fails
Sole-source on the cheapest modelPricing changes; quality regresses release-to-release. Always have a fallback prompt-tested on a second vendor.
"We'll fine-tune so we can switch later"Fine-tunes are not portable across vendors. Prompts + RAG are.
"It's just an API call"Tool-use, function-calling, and structured output formats differ. Real switching cost is in the surrounding code.
Ignoring the cloud-aggregator pathBedrock / Vertex / Azure OpenAI inherit your existing DPA, BAA, and procurement. Often faster to ship than a direct contract.
Buying frontier when small would doMid-tier models (GPT-4o-mini, Claude Haiku) hit the threshold for ~80% of enterprise tasks at a fraction of the cost. Always benchmark mid-tier first; escalate to frontier only when evals demand it.
